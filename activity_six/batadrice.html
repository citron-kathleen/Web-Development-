Batad Rice Terraces



About the Batad Rice Terraces:

The Batad Rice Terraces are spectacular, ancient, hand-carved terraces in Ifugao, Philippines, built by the indigenous Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago as part of a UNESCO World Heritage site. They resemble a grand amphitheater and are sustained by a unique, functional, and ancient irrigation system that draws water from mountain springs and forests. Accessing the site requires a hike through a lush forest to reach the viewpoints, with the best views available during the planting (January) and harvest (March or April) seasons.
