Fort Santiago



About the Fort Santiago:

Fort Santiago is a historic citadel within Intramuros, Manila, originally built by the Spanish in 1571 as a defensive fortress and key trading point. It is best known as the last prison of the Philippine national hero Jose Rizal before his execution in 1896, and now serves as a museum and memorial site to his life and legacy. The fort also contains dungeons where many lives were lost during the Spanish colonial period and World War II, making it a significant site of Philippine history and culture.
